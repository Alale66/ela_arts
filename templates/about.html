Engineering, Art, and the Space Between

I’m Elahe Sadeghi — a software developer, illustrator, and maker of small handmade worlds. My work lives at the intersection of logic and imagination: one hand writing code, the other sketching characters, building puppets, or shaping stories frame by frame.

I’ve spent more than a decade developing software in C#, SQL, Python, and JavaScript, building everything from enterprise applications to mobile tools and backend services. Alongside that technical path, I’ve always carried a parallel creative life — drawing, painting, crafting, and exploring stop‑motion animation.

Ela Arts is where these two worlds finally meet. Here, I build systems and stories with the same intention: clarity, emotion, and a sense of play. Whether I’m engineering a modular gallery layout or illustrating a children’s book character, I’m always searching for that moment where technology feels human and art feels alive.

This space is a growing archive of my work — the code I write, the characters I create, and the handmade pieces that connect them.
